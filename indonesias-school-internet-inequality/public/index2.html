Indonesia School Internet Inequality

By : Lamanisa Rasid Saputra

Introduction

Indonesia continues to grapple with a significant digital divide in education, with many schools still lacking internet access. The gap is especially stark between urban centers and remote regions while schools in major cities are mostly connected, those in isolated provinces often remain offline. This disparity severely limits learning opportunities for millions of students, particularly in public schools, which tend to lag behind their private counterparts.

This project aims to bring these inequalities to light through visual storytelling, encouraging data driven policy responses. By using interactive choropleth maps and small multiples, we will illustrate regional differences and compare internet access across various school levels, from elementary to special education. Users will be able to explore the public-private divide through dynamic filters, while tooltips will offer detailed insights such as unmet needs in each province.
